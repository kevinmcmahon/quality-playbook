# BUG-012 — Zero-bug sentinel matches "zero" anywhere in prose

## Requirement
REQ-015 (Zero-bug sentinel uses anchored structure). The zero-bug sentinel in
`check_bugs_heading` must use an anchored structural check, not a free substring
search for "zero" or "0 confirmed".
Doc source: `[source]` — EXPLORATION.md §QR-8.

## Candidate origin
Source stage: Phase 1 exploration / Pass 1 structural review.
Candidate bug ID: CB-12.

## Location
- File: `.github/skills/quality_gate/quality_gate.py`
- Lines: 353–411 (zero-bug sentinel at line 397)
- Function(s): `check_bugs_heading`

## Symptom
A `BUGS.md` file reading "here is the zero analysis of our project" (with no BUG headings
and no proper zero-bug declaration) passes the zero-bug sentinel check. The gate reports
`bug_count = 0` and proceeds without warning. A genuinely empty BUGS.md (no content
written yet) with accidental "zero" prose would also pass.

## Root cause
The sentinel regex at line 397 is `re.search(r"(No confirmed|zero|0 confirmed)", bugs_content)`.
The word `zero` is a common English word and matches anywhere in prose. The regex is not
anchored to a heading structure; a `## No confirmed bugs` heading is not required.
